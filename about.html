About the Platform

Our Mission

The Public Health Emergency Data Platform is committed to providing timely, accurate, and data-driven insights to support public health emergency preparedness and response. By integrating information from diverse sources—including surveillance systems, laboratory reports, demographic data, and environmental indicators—the platform transforms complex datasets into actionable intelligence. This enables public health professionals, policymakers, and humanitarian responders to monitor emerging risks, track disease trends, and identify priority areas for intervention. Our mission emphasizes not only the collection of data but also the translation of information into clear, practical insights that inform rapid decision-making and resource allocation during emergencies..

Our Vision

We envision a world where communities are resilient and public health systems are proactive in preventing, detecting, and responding to health emergencies. The platform aims to strengthen evidence-based decision-making, foster collaboration across agencies, and enhance global health preparedness. By providing accessible, reliable, and actionable information, we seek to empower stakeholders at all levels to respond effectively to outbreaks, mitigate risks, and protect vulnerable populations. Through innovation, transparency, and a commitment to data integrity, our vision is to build a trusted, comprehensive tool that contributes to safer and healthier societies worldwide. This platform underscores the importance of bridging the gap between data and action, ensuring that accurate information is not only available but also actionable, fostering resilient health systems and informed communities capable of navigating public health emergencies with confidence and efficiency.

Data Sources

The primary source of data for the platform comes from the member states of the WHO African Region (AFRO), comprising 46 countries. Each country collects health information through its Health Management Information System (HMIS), starting from the health service delivery points at the community or facility level and progressing through district, zonal, and regional health bureaus before reaching the national Ministry of Health. Reporting is conducted in a timely manner, with frequency determined by the nature and urgency of the disease. For instance, immediately notifiable diseases—including polio, Ebola, Marburg, and other epidemic-prone diseases—are reported on a daily basis to ensure rapid response and containment. Cases that exceed established epidemic thresholds, such as measles or other vaccine-preventable diseases (VPDs), also receive immediate attention. Other communicable and non-communicable diseases are reported on a weekly basis following the hierarchical structure of health delivery points, from local facilities to districts, zones, regions, and ultimately the national HMIS database. At each administrative level, health authorities perform data analysis based on catchment population and geographic area, enabling targeted monitoring of disease trends, risk assessment, and resource allocation. This structured reporting and analytical process ensures that data-driven insights are available for public health emergency preparedness, response, and strategic planning across the African region..

Our Impact

The Public Health Emergency Data Platform plays a critical role in enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of public health decision-making. By integrating real-time data from multiple sources across member states, the platform allows policymakers, health authorities, and humanitarian responders to identify disease hotspots, monitor trends, and detect emerging health threats quickly. This timely access to actionable information enables rapid intervention, helping to prevent the spread of infectious diseases and mitigate public health emergencies before they escalate. Through interactive dashboards, spatial mapping, and trend analysis, the platform transforms complex datasets into clear, actionable insights. Decision-makers can analyze patterns across regions, compare disease incidence over time, and prioritize resources for areas of greatest need. The ability to detect anomalies or unusual spikes in disease incidence empowers health teams to respond proactively rather than reactively, reducing morbidity and mortality associated with outbreaks. Moreover, the platform supports strategic planning and coordination among public health stakeholders by providing a shared, trusted source of data. It strengthens preparedness by highlighting vulnerable populations, high-risk areas, and potential resource gaps, enabling targeted interventions. Over time, this evidence-based approach has the potential to enhance resilience, improve emergency response efficiency, and ultimately save lives. In essence, the platform’s impact is measured not only in data and analytics but in its ability to inform timely, informed, and coordinated action—making it an indispensable tool for managing public health emergencies and protecting communities.
